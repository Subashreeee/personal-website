Fake News Detector:

Introduction

Fake news is intentionally written to mislead readers to believe false information. Fake news is untrue information presented as news. It frequently aims to make money through advertising or to harm someone's or an organization's reputation. The prevalence of fake news has grown along with social media. The identification of fake news is becoming more and more challenging because people with malicious purpose are generating fake news articles that are difficult to distinguish from legitimate news. Machine-driven identification of fake news is essential since people typically lack the time to check references and ensure that reports are authentic. What we have done is a simplistic approach that looks at the news headlines and tries to predict whether they may be fake or not. Fake news can be intimidating as they attract more audience than normal.

Problem Statement

A major challenge in fake news detection is to detect it in the early phase. Another challenge in fake news detection is the unavailability or the shortage of labelled data for training the detection models. We propose a novel fake news detection framework that can address these challenges. Our proposed framework exploits the information from the news articles and the social contexts to detect fake news

Aim of the Project

The aim of the project is to develop a fake news detector to avoid and detect the fake news spread on the internet.

General architecture

The above figure represents the architecture diagram of this project. Firstly, the dataset is obtained and pre-processed.
